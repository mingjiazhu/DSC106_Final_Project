What are the Most Important Traits of a Happy Country?

The World Happiness Report ranks countries based on how happy a citizen is with themselves in a country. The World Happiness Report has several categories that surveys citizens in order to get a uniform score of the country’s happiness. In the collection of World Happiness Report from 2015 to 2019, the top 6 factors of happiness that explains for higher ranking among countries are GDP per capita, social support, health life expectancy, freedom to make life choices, generosity, and perceptions of corruption. However, we expect that despite the change in years GDP per capita, social scores, and life expectancy score will consistently be the major impact in a country’s happiness score. In this website, we will experiment to see which factors affect the happiness of a country by comparing the 2015’s report to 2019’s report.

In 2015, GDP per capita and social support covered roughly equal amounts as the best attribute of happiness score all over the world. But in 2019, social support has overtaken most of the countries in the world.

Conclusion

Pie charts that show the weights of each factor in each year


In 2015, GDP per capita, social support, and life expectancy attributes cover approximately 45% of the happiness scores for all countries. In 2019, GDP per capita, social support, and life expectancy attributes cover more than half of the happiness scores for all countries.

As we expected, there are no changes of the best attributes in happiness score despite the change in years. The best attributes continue to cover for the majority of the happiness scores as the years go by. We conclude that high scores in GDP per capita, social support, and life expectancy categories to be the best indicators for a happy country.
